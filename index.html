01 · About the Project

Charging that happens while you drive, not while you wait.

Electric vehicles are only as convenient as the plug they depend on. This project removes the plug entirely, embedding charging infrastructure directly into the road surface.

Concept

What is wireless charging?

Energy transfers from a transmitter coil buried in the road to a receiver coil on the vehicle through resonant magnetic induction — no physical contact, no cables, no exposed connectors.

Why EVs

Why electric vehicles need it

Range anxiety and charging downtime remain the biggest barriers to EV adoption. Charging a moving vehicle removes both problems by turning the commute itself into charge time.

Impact

Why this technology matters

Smaller batteries, lighter vehicles, longer range, and roads that actively power the transport network — this is the infrastructure layer the electric era is still missing.

02 · Working Principle

From grid electricity to a charged battery, in five stages.

01

Electricity enters the transmitter coil

AC power from the grid is fed into copper transmitter coils embedded beneath the road surface.

02

A magnetic field is generated

Alternating current in the coil produces an oscillating magnetic field that radiates upward through the road surface.

03

The receiver coil captures energy

A matching coil mounted under the vehicle sits within range of the field and captures the transferred energy via electromagnetic induction.

04

Power is converted into DC

A rectifier circuit on board the vehicle converts the induced alternating current into stable direct current.

05

The battery charges

Regulated DC output flows into the vehicle's battery management system, topping up charge continuously while in motion.
